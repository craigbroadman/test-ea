Date and Time Format (Standards)

Table of Contents
  • Standard
  • Useful resources

As an international company we should use and promote the use of an internal standard for dates to avoid confusion that arises when writing dates in US vs UK formats.

For example: someone in the US will read 10/07/2022 as October 7th 2022, however in the UK it is read as 10th July 2022.

We should adopt a standard and roll it out across architecture documents, then attempt to push this across the whole business.

Standard

In general, spell out the names of months and days of the week in full. Give the full four-digit year, not a two-digit abbreviation.

✅ Recommended: January 19, 2017

If including the day of the week, add it before the month as follows: DAY_OF_WEEK, MONTH DAY, YEAR.

✅ Recommended: Tuesday, April 27, 2021

If you must express a date in numerical date format, use the format YYYY-MM-DD, and separate the elements by using hyphens. This conforms to ISO 8601 international standards for numerical date format.

✅ "YYYY-MM-DD" (ISO 8601).
