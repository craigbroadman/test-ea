ℹ Solution Architects should be conducting and recording architecture reviews

  • Status: Accepted

  • Deciders: Matthew Collyer, Craig Broadman, Mike Kocher, Wayne Ennis, Rob Wetzel, Ben Blewett, Dirk VanderBent

  • Date: 2022-08-31

Context and Problem Statement

As an Enterprise Architecture function we should have visibility of architecture reviews taking place across product teams.

An architecture review is looking at a proposed design to assess if it conforms with iPipeline patterns and whether there is already an existing solution that could be reused.

We should make a record of them taking place and their findings.

Decision Drivers

  • Quality through consistency

  • Re-use by design

  • Technical debt is understood

  • Findings are visible to the Enterprise Architecture group

  • The Enterprise Architecture group can offer assistance and step in if necessary

Considered Options

  • Who should be involved in the process?

  • Approval vs assessment

  • Technology choices

Decision Outcome

Who should be involved?

Primarily Solution Architectures who are subject matter experts for their products. However, the ability to escalate a review to Enterprise Architects will be made available

Approval vs assessment

We do not want architecture to become an ivory tower of "thou must do this" and "thou must do that", therefore all architecture reviews should be treated as asssessments with findings being recorded and categorised accordingly.

Technology choices

  • *Microsoft Forms will be used to request a review to take place as this hooks into the Microsoft eco system and processes can be automated through the use of Microsoft Power Automate

  • On submission of the form, the appropriate archtitects will be notified via Microsoft Teams with an adaptive card that must be completed to capture findings

  • On submission the findings will be recorded in a Microsoft Teams list

  • The findings must be documented in this repo
